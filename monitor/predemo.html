Dangerous Demo - ClueCon 2025

Asterisk Versus FreeSWITCH Stress Test

Overview

In this live and intense Dangerous Demo at ClueCon 2025, we put two of the most powerful open-source VoIP engines to the test: Asterisk and FreeSWITCH. Our goal is to measure and compare their performance under extreme SIP call loads.

Installation Details

Both platforms were compiled and installed from their latest source code to ensure maximum performance and flexibility:

  • Asterisk: Built from the latest source with Channel Storage Backend Optimization enabled.
  • FreeSWITCH: Compiled from the most recent source tree, with all modern improvements.

SIP Stack Comparison

For SIP communication, each system utilizes its own core SIP stack:

  • Asterisk: uses PJSIP, known for its modular and efficient SIP handling.
  • FreeSWITCH: uses Sofia-SIP, a robust and flexible SIP engine built for scalability.

Stress Test Purpose

The test is designed to push both systems to their limits by generating high volumes of concurrent SIP calls. Our goal is to benchmark the performance, responsiveness, and stability of both PJSIP and Sofia under pressure.

Metrics gathered include CPU usage, memory consumption, call handling efficiency, and crash resistance.

Conclusion

This battle of SIP titans offers valuable insights into the architecture and runtime performance of two industry-leading platforms. Whether you're team Asterisk or team FreeSWITCH, this test showcases the power, limits, and resilience of open-source communication systems.
